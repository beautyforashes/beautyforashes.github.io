Introduction

What is a theory of atonement and why do we use it? How does a given model filter and shape our experience with God and the church? How does our dominant model influence the way we repent, we forgive, we work, and we serve?

Our primary goal will be to explore the models which men have created to try and understand the atonement of Jesus Christ: both what he did for us and how his salvation works.

Theses

  • Jesus Christ atoned for us. What this means has actually been described by a whole panoply of words and notions embedded in theology, culture, and language.
  • Our models of atonement generally have their roots in particular socioeconomic or cultural practices which we use as a lens to understand God’s action.
  • The model of atonement invoked in a particular discursive setting tends to govern the extent to which we view Christ and ourselves as bearing the burdens of sin.
  • The latter-day gospel as revealed by Joseph Smith has provided fruitful ground for speculation and reconstruction of what, exactly, it was that Jesus did for us in the atonement.
