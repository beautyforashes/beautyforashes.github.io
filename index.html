Introduction

What is a theory of atonement and why do we use it? How does a given model filter and shape our experience with God and the church? How does our dominant model influence the way we repent, we forgive, we work, and we serve?

This is a celebration of Easter. Our primary goal is to explore the models which men have created to try and understand the atonement of Jesus Christ: both what he did for us and how his salvation works. We examine historical theological proposals as well as unique Latter Day Saint theories in order to find out what we may.

Theses

  • Jesus Christ atoned for us. What this actually means has been described by a whole panoply of words and notions embedded in theology, culture, and language.
  • Models of atonement generally have deep roots in particular socioeconomic or cultural practices which are used as a lens to understand God’s action.
  • The model of atonement invoked in a particular setting can influence the relationship we experience with Christ as our Savior, particularly as to how the burden of sin is borne.
  • The latter-day gospel as revealed by Joseph Smith has provided fruitful ground for speculation and reconstruction of what, exactly, it was that Jesus did for us in the atonement.
